General Nutrition Guidelines for Weight Loss

  • Create a caloric deficit of 500-750 calories per day for safe weight loss (0.5-1 kg per week)
  • Focus on whole, unprocessed foods - vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, whole grains
  • Increase protein intake to 1.2-1.6g per kg body weight to preserve muscle mass
  • Stay hydrated - drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily
  • Limit added sugars and refined carbohydrates
  • Include healthy fats from nuts, seeds, avocados, and olive oil
  • Practice portion control - use smaller plates and mindful eating
  • Eat regular meals - don't skip breakfast
  • Reduce sodium intake to less than 2,300mg per day
  • Include fiber-rich foods (25-30g daily) for satiety

Foods to Prioritize

  • Leafy greens: spinach, kale, lettuce
  • Lean proteins: chicken breast, fish, tofu, legumes
  • Whole grains: brown rice, quinoa, oats
  • Low-fat dairy or alternatives
  • Fresh fruits (in moderation)
  • Non-starchy vegetables

Foods to Limit or Avoid

  • Sugary beverages and sodas
  • Fried and processed foods
  • White bread, pastries, and refined grains
  • High-fat dairy products
  • Processed meats
  • Excessive alcohol consumption

Treatment Guidelines

Candidates for Drug Therapy:

  • BMI 27-29.9 kg/m¬≤ with one or more weight-related comorbidities
  • Have not achieved 5% weight loss with lifestyle interventions alone after 3-6 months

Treatment Goals: 5% weight loss is considered effective; medications are typically used long-term as obesity is a chronic condition

First-Line Medications (GLP-1 Based Therapies - Most Effective)

Tirzepatide (Zepbound) - PREFERRED FIRST-LINE

Type: Dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist (Most effective agent available)

Mechanism: Increases satiety, slows gastric emptying, improves glucose-dependent insulin secretion

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with comorbidities; Also approved for moderate-to-severe obstructive sleep apnea

Dosage: Injectable, once weekly. Start 2.5mg, increase to 5mg, up to maximum 15mg weekly

Expected Weight Loss: 15-22.5% of body weight (highest efficacy)

Additional Benefits: Improves sleep apnea, glycemic control, cardiovascular risk factors

Clinical Note: Superior efficacy compared to all other agents in head-to-head trials

Semaglutide (Wegovy) - PREFERRED FIRST-LINE

Type: GLP-1 receptor agonist

Mechanism: Reduces appetite, increases satiety, slows gastric emptying

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with comorbidities; Also approved for cardiovascular risk reduction in patients with established CVD

Dosage: Injectable, once weekly. Titrate from 0.25mg up to 2.4mg weekly over 16 weeks

Expected Weight Loss: 10-15% of body weight

Cardiovascular Benefits: Reduces major adverse cardiovascular events by 20% in patients with established CVD

Additional Approvals: Treatment of MASH (metabolic-associated steatohepatitis) with liver fibrosis

Preferred for: Patients with cardiovascular disease or at high CV risk

Liraglutide (Saxenda)

Type: GLP-1 receptor agonist (first approved GLP-1 for obesity)

Mechanism: Appetite suppression, increases satiety

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with weight-related conditions

Dosage: Injectable, once daily. Titrate from 0.6mg to 3.0mg daily over 5 weeks

Expected Weight Loss: 5-10% of body weight

Clinical Note: Less effective than semaglutide and tirzepatide; daily injection required

GLP-1 Medication Important Information

Common Side Effects: Nausea (25-40%), vomiting, diarrhea, constipation (usually improve with time)

Management Tips: Eat smaller meals, eat slowly, avoid high-fat/greasy foods, slower dose titration if needed

Contraindications:

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid cancer or MEN 2A/2B
  • Pregnancy or planning pregnancy
  • Severe gastroparesis or inflammatory bowel disease

Pre-Surgery: May need to be held before elective surgery due to delayed gastric emptying

Long-term Use: Weight regain occurs if medication is stopped; typically used indefinitely

Second-Line Medications (Alternatives if GLP-1 not suitable)

Phentermine-Topiramate (Qsymia)

Type: Combination appetite suppressant

Mechanism: Phentermine (sympathomimetic) + Topiramate (anticonvulsant) - suppresses appetite, increases satiety

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with comorbidities (without cardiovascular disease or uncontrolled hypertension)

Dosage: Oral, once daily. Start 3.75/23mg, increase to 7.5/46mg, up to max 15/92mg

Expected Weight Loss: 8-10% of body weight

Additional Benefits: May help with migraine prevention (topiramate component)

Important Warnings: Teratogenic - requires monthly pregnancy testing; avoid in cardiovascular disease

Contraindications: Pregnancy, glaucoma, hyperthyroidism, uncontrolled hypertension, cardiovascular disease

Naltrexone-Bupropion (Contrave)

Type: Combination opioid antagonist + antidepressant

Mechanism: Affects brain reward pathways and appetite regulation through POMC neurons

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with comorbidities

Dosage: Oral, twice daily. Titrate from 1 tablet daily to 2 tablets twice daily over 4 weeks

Expected Weight Loss: 4-6% of body weight

Potential Benefits: May help with depression, smoking cessation, alcohol use disorder

Side Effects: Nausea (30%), headache, constipation, insomnia; monitor for depression/suicidal thoughts

Contraindications: Seizure disorders, bulimia, chronic opioid use, uncontrolled hypertension, pregnancy

Note: Cardiovascular safety not fully established

Phentermine (Adipex-P) - Short-term Use

Type: Sympathomimetic appetite suppressant

Mechanism: Increases energy expenditure and suppresses appetite

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with comorbidities (typically for 12 weeks, though some use long-term)

Dosage: Oral, 15-37.5mg daily or 30mg diffuse-controlled release

Expected Weight Loss: 5-8% of body weight

Important: Schedule IV controlled substance; can increase heart rate and blood pressure

Contraindications: Cardiovascular disease, uncontrolled hypertension, hyperthyroidism, substance use disorder

Orlistat (Xenical/Alli)

Type: Lipase inhibitor (alters fat digestion)

Mechanism: Blocks absorption of approximately 30% of dietary fat

Indication: BMI ‚â•30 or BMI ‚â•27 with comorbidities; Safe for cardiovascular disease patients

Dosage: Oral, 120mg three times daily with meals (60mg OTC version available)

Expected Weight Loss: 5-8% of body weight (3kg more than placebo)

Benefits: Reduces diabetes risk, improves lipid profile, lowers blood pressure

Side Effects: Gastrointestinal (oily stools, fecal urgency, flatulence) - improves with low-fat diet

Requirement: Take multivitamin at bedtime (can decrease fat-soluble vitamins)

Preferred for: Patients with cardiovascular disease where stimulants contraindicated

Investigational Agents (Under Research)

Several promising medications are in clinical trials:

  • Cagrilintide + Semaglutide: Combination therapy showing 20% weight loss
  • Retatrutide: Triple hormone agonist with up to 24% weight loss
  • Maridebart cafraglutide (MariTide): Once-monthly injection
  • Oral Semaglutide 50mg: Once-daily pill with 15% weight loss
  • Orforglipron: Daily oral GLP-1 with 11% weight loss

Monitoring & Follow-up Requirements

  • Initial Phase: Follow-up every 4-6 weeks for dose titration and side effect assessment
  • Ongoing: Every 2-3 months to monitor weight, blood pressure, heart rate
  • For Diabetes Patients: Monitor blood glucose, adjust insulin/sulfonylureas to prevent hypoglycemia
  • Discontinuation Criteria: If <4-5% weight loss after 12 weeks at maximum tolerated dose
  • Long-term Treatment: Most patients require indefinite medication use to maintain weight loss
